Buying in Belize

Your comprehensive guide to making Belize your new home. From residency programs to understanding the cost of living, we've got you covered.

Belize Relocation Guide & QRP Program

Belize offers a straightforward path to residency, especially for retirees, through its Qualified Retired Persons (QRP) program. This program is designed for individuals aged 45 and over who can demonstrate a monthly income of at least $2,000 USD from a pension, annuity, or other source outside of Belize. [1, 2, 3]

Key benefits of the QRP program include a complete tax exemption on all income derived from sources outside of Belize. [2, 3] Additionally, QRP members can import their personal and household effects, as well as a personal vehicle, duty-free during their first year of residency. [2, 3] This makes the transition to living in Belize significantly more affordable and hassle-free.

Cost of Living in Belize

The cost of living in Belize is generally lower than in North America and Europe, though it varies by location. [4, 5] Coastal areas like Placencia and Ambergris Caye tend to be more expensive, while inland regions offer a more budget-friendly lifestyle. [4]

A couple can live comfortably on a budget of approximately $2,500 to $3,000 USD per month. [4, 5] This estimate typically covers housing, utilities, groceries, transportation, and healthcare. Locally sourced goods, such as fresh produce and seafood, are very affordable, while imported items carry a higher price tag. [5] The Belizean dollar is pegged to the US dollar at a fixed 2:1 ratio, which simplifies financial planning for expatriates. [4]
